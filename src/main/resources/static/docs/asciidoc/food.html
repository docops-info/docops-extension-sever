2. Introduction

Create beautiful, structured cooking recipes with ingredients, steps, and notes

Key features:

  • Clear recipe metadata: title, yield, prep time, cook time

  • Structured sections for tags, summary, ingredients, steps, and notes

  • Supports multiline content for readable recipe authoring

  • Theme support for light and dark presentations

  • Ideal for cooking docs, meal plans, and food blogs

3. Default Example

Basic recipe for a chocolate cake:

[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
title=Classic Pancakes
yield=4 servings
prep=10 min
cook=15 min
tags=breakfast, sweet, quick
summary=Fluffy pancakes with a golden crust and a soft, tender center.

ingredients=
- 1 1/2 cups flour
- 3 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 1/4 cups milk
- 1 egg
- 3 tbsp butter, melted

steps=
1. Sift flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar into a large bowl.
2. Make a well in the center and add milk, egg, and melted butter.
3. Stir until the batter is smooth and just combined.
4. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or pan over medium-high heat.
5. Pour batter onto the hot surface and cook until golden on both sides.

notes=
- Do not overmix the batter.
- Let the pan fully preheat before cooking.
- Add blueberries or chocolate chips if desired.
----

4. Focused Examples

4.1. Simple Weeknight Pasta

[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
theme=ayu
 Weeknight Pasta
yield= 2 servings
prep= 10 minutes
cook= 15 minutes
tags= pasta, quick, vegetarian
summary=
A fast, flavorful pasta dish for busy evenings.
ingredients=
- 200g pasta
- 2 cloves garlic
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 tsp chili flakes
- Salt to taste
steps=
1. Cook the pasta until al dente.
2. Sauté garlic in olive oil.
3. Toss pasta with chili flakes and salt.
4. Serve immediately.
notes=
- Add parmesan for extra flavor.
- Use gluten-free pasta if needed.
----

6. Format Options

6.1. Basic Structure

[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
 Recipe Title
yield= 4 servings
prep= 15 minutes
cook= 30 minutes
tags= tag1, tag2, tag3
summary= Short description of the recipe.
ingredients=
- Ingredient 1
- Ingredient 2
steps=
1. First step
2. Second step
notes=
- Optional note
theme= classic
----

6.2. Supported Fields

Field Purpose

title

Recipe name shown at the top

yield

Serving size or batch yield

prep

Preparation time

cook

Cooking time

tags

Comma- or semicolon-separated tags

summary

Short description of the recipe

ingredients

List of ingredients

steps

Ordered or unordered step list

notes

Additional recipe notes or tips

theme

Theme name used for styling

6.3. List Formatting Rules

  • tags= accepts comma-separated or semicolon-separated values

  • ingredients= accepts bullet lists or plain lines

  • steps= accepts numbered lists or bullet lists

  • notes= accepts bullet lists or plain lines

Example:

ingredients=
- 2 cups flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 eggs

steps=
1. Preheat oven to 180C.
2. Mix ingredients.
3. Bake for 30 minutes.

🔝

7. Best Practices

  • Keep titles short and clear - Use the recipe name, not a full sentence

  • Use consistent yield formatting - For example, 4 servings or 1 loaf

  • Keep steps concise - One action per step improves readability

  • Prefer bullet ingredients - Makes scanning easier in the rendered output

  • Add useful notes - Include substitutions, storage tips, or serving suggestions

  • Use tags for discovery - Help readers find vegetarian, quick, dessert, or seasonal recipes

For best results, keep ingredients in the order they are used and keep steps short and action-oriented. If a recipe has special preparation notes, put them in notes= so the main steps stay clean.
