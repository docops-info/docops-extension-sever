== Introduction

_Create beautiful, structured cooking recipes with ingredients, steps, and notes_

*Key features:*

* Clear recipe metadata: title, yield, prep time, cook time
* Structured sections for tags, summary, ingredients, steps, and notes
* Supports multiline content for readable recipe authoring
* Theme support for light and dark presentations
* Ideal for cooking docs, meal plans, and food blogs

[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
theme=everest
 Mexican Street Corn (Bartaco )
yield= side / dip / taco topping
prep= 10 minutes
cook= 15 minutes
tags= Mexican, corn, side dish, dip, tacos, easy
summary=
A bright, creamy, and lightly spicy street corn inspired by Bartaco. Great as a side, dip, or taco topping.
ingredients=
- 5 ears of corn (or 3 cans of fire-roasted corn)
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 3 limes, juiced (approximately 6 Tbsp)
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1/4 cup cotija cheese, crumbled or finely grated
- 1 Tbsp fresh chopped cilantro
steps=
1. If grilling the corn, pull the husks back off the kernels, leaving them attached for easier handling.
2. Grill over medium heat for 2-3 minutes per side, about 15 minutes total, until slightly charred.
3. Let the corn cool, then cut the kernels off the cobs.
4. Place a saucepan over medium heat and mix together the corn, sour cream, lime juice, and cayenne.
5. Warm for 5 minutes.
6. Add cilantro and cotija cheese just before serving.
notes=
- Works well as a side, dip with chips, or taco topping.
- If you want the easy version, use canned fire-roasted corn instead of grilling fresh corn.
- Add more lime or cayenne to taste.
----

[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
 Garlic Naan
yield= about 8 naans
prep= 20 minutes (+1 hour rise)
cook= 15 minutes
tags= bread, naan, garlic, homemade, Indian-inspired
summary=
Soft homemade garlic naan made with yogurt, yeast, and a buttery garlic-parsley finish.

ingredients=
- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1 cup yogurt
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3/4 cup water (warm)
- 1 packet yeast (7g)
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 stick butter, melted
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- Parsley, chopped

steps=
1. In a small bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
2. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda.
3. Add yogurt, olive oil, and the yeast mixture to the dry ingredients. Mix into a soft dough.
4. Knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rise for about 1 hour, or until doubled.
5. Divide dough into equal balls and roll each into an oval or circle.
6. Cook each naan on a hot skillet/tawa for 1–2 minutes per side until puffed and charred spots appear.
7. Mix melted butter with chopped garlic and parsley.
8. Brush hot naan with garlic butter and serve warm.

notes=
- If dough feels sticky, dust lightly with flour while kneading.
- Use medium-high heat for best bubbles and char.
- Best served fresh with curry or dips.
theme= reo
----

== Format Options

=== Basic Structure
[source,asciidoc]
....
[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
 Recipe Title
yield= 4 servings
prep= 15 minutes
cook= 30 minutes
tags= tag1, tag2, tag3
summary= Short description of the recipe.
ingredients=
- Ingredient 1
- Ingredient 2
steps=
1. First step
2. Second step
notes=
- Optional note
theme= classic
----
....

=== Supported Fields

[cols="1,3"]
|===
|Field |Purpose

|title
|Recipe name shown at the top

|yield
|Serving size or batch yield

|prep
|Preparation time

|cook
|Cooking time

|tags
|Comma- or semicolon-separated tags

|summary
|Short description of the recipe

|ingredients
|List of ingredients

|steps
|Ordered or unordered step list

|notes
|Additional recipe notes or tips

|theme
|Theme name used for styling
|===

=== List Formatting Rules

* `tags=` accepts comma-separated or semicolon-separated values
* `ingredients=` accepts bullet lists or plain lines
* `steps=` accepts numbered lists or bullet lists
* `notes=` accepts bullet lists or plain lines

Example:

[source]
----
ingredients=
- 2 cups flour
- 1 cup sugar
- 2 eggs

steps=
1. Preheat oven to 180C.
2. Mix ingredients.
3. Bake for 30 minutes.
----

== Best Practices

* *Keep titles short and clear* - Use the recipe name, not a full sentence
* *Use consistent yield formatting* - For example, `4 servings` or `1 loaf`
* *Keep steps concise* - One action per step improves readability
* *Prefer bullet ingredients* - Makes scanning easier in the rendered output
* *Add useful notes* - Include substitutions, storage tips, or serving suggestions
* *Use tags for discovery* - Help readers find vegetarian, quick, dessert, or seasonal recipes

[TIP]
====
For best results, keep ingredients in the order they are used and keep steps short and action-oriented. If a recipe has special preparation notes, put them in `notes=` so the main steps stay clean.
====
