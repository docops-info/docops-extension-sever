=== Garlic Naan
[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
theme=everest
title=Garlic Naan
yield= about 8 naans
prep= 20 minutes (+1 hour rise)
cook= 15 minutes
tags= bread, naan, garlic, homemade, Indian-inspired
summary=
Soft homemade garlic naan made with yogurt, yeast, and a buttery garlic-parsley finish.

ingredients=
- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp baking soda
- 1 cup yogurt
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3/4 cup water (warm)
- 1 packet yeast (7g)
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 stick butter, melted
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- Parsley, chopped

steps=
1. In a small bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
2. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda.
3. Add yogurt, olive oil, and the yeast mixture to the dry ingredients. Mix into a soft dough.
4. Knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rise for about 1 hour, or until doubled.
5. Divide dough into equal balls and roll each into an oval or circle.
6. Cook each naan on a hot skillet/tawa for 1–2 minutes per side until puffed and charred spots appear.
7. Mix melted butter with chopped garlic and parsley.
8. Brush hot naan with garlic butter and serve warm.

notes=
- If dough feels sticky, dust lightly with flour while kneading.
- Use medium-high heat for best bubbles and char.
- Best served fresh with curry or dips.
----

=== Mexican Street Corn (Bartaco)
[docops,recipe,controls="true"]
----
theme=everest
title=Mexican Street Corn (Bartaco )
yield= side / dip / taco topping
prep= 10 minutes
cook= 15 minutes
tags= Mexican, corn, side dish, dip, tacos, easy
summary=
A bright, creamy, and lightly spicy street corn inspired by Bartaco. Great as a side, dip, or taco topping.
ingredients=
- 5 ears of corn (or 3 cans of fire-roasted corn)
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 3 limes, juiced (approximately 6 Tbsp)
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1/4 cup cotija cheese, crumbled or finely grated
- 1 Tbsp fresh chopped cilantro
steps=
1. If grilling the corn, pull the husks back off the kernels, leaving them attached for easier handling.
2. Grill over medium heat for 2-3 minutes per side, about 15 minutes total, until slightly charred.
3. Let the corn cool, then cut the kernels off the cobs.
4. Place a saucepan over medium heat and mix together the corn, sour cream, lime juice, and cayenne.
5. Warm for 5 minutes.
6. Add cilantro and cotija cheese just before serving.
notes=
- Works well as a side, dip with chips, or taco topping.
- If you want the easy version, use canned fire-roasted corn instead of grilling fresh corn.
- Add more lime or cayenne to taste.
----

title= Roasted Vegetable Soup
yield= 4 servings
prep= 15 minutes
cook= 35 minutes
tags= soup, seasonal, healthy
summary=
A warm roasted vegetable soup with a smooth, rich texture.
ingredients=
- 2 carrots
- 1 onion
- 1 zucchini
- 2 tomatoes
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cups vegetable stock
- Salt and pepper to taste
steps=
1. Roast the vegetables until tender.
2. Transfer to a pot and add stock.
3. Simmer for 10 minutes.
