title=Coconut Choka (Non-Traditional)
yield=4 servings
prep=10 min
cook=15 min
tags=Guyanese, coconut, side dish, vegetarian
summary=A non-traditional take on coconut choka using a food processor instead of the traditional sil and lorha. Roasted coconut shreds ground with garlic and pepper for a smooth, fragrant accompaniment to dhal and rice.

ingredients=
- 1 bag 365 Whole Foods Market Coconut Shredded Organic
- 2 to 4 wiri wiri peppers or 1 scotch bonnet or 1 habanero pepper
- 1 to 2 pieces of garlic
- salt to taste
- some water

steps=
1. Place a large skillet on low heat on the stove top.
2. Pour coconut shreds into the skillet and add salt to taste.
3. Keep stirring the coconut on low heat until most shreds are browned and fragrant. Do not burn.
4. Remove from heat and let cool for a few minutes.
5. Pour the roasted coconut shreds into a food processor.
6. Add garlic, peppers, a bit of water, and salt if needed.
7. Turn on the food processor and add water gradually until the coconut reaches a smooth texture.
8. Turn off the food processor and empty the coconut choka into a bowl.
9. Serve with dhal and rice and enjoy.

notes=
- Keep heat low to avoid burning the coconut.
- A food processor is used instead of the traditional sil and lorha.
- Add water gradually for the right texture.
- The roasted coconut flavor with pepper makes this dish excellent even without the traditional method.
