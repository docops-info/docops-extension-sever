title=Shrimp Scampi
yield=4 servings
prep=15 minutes
cook=20 minutes
tags=Italian-American, seafood, pasta, garlic
summary=
A classic Italian-American dish with plump, garlicky shrimp in a rich white wine butter sauce over pasta.

ingredients=
- 340 grams linguine or spaghetti
- 680 grams large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/2 cups dry white wine (e.g. Pinot Grigio)
- 1/3 cups chicken or seafood broth
- 1 lemon, juiced
- 1/2 teaspoons red pepper flakes
- 1 teaspoons salt
- 1/2 teaspoons black pepper
- 1/3 cups fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 lemon, sliced (for serving)
- 1/3 cups parmesan, grated (optional)

steps=
1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook 340 grams linguine or spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup pasta water, then drain and set aside.
2. Season the shrimp: Pat 680 grams large shrimp, peeled and deveined dry with paper towels. Season with 1 teaspoons salt and 1/2 teaspoons black pepper. Dry shrimp = better sear.
3. Sear the shrimp: Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook until pink and just opaque, about 1–2 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate — don't overcook!
4. Build the sauce: Reduce heat to medium. Add 4 tablespoons unsalted butter to the same pan. Once melted, add 6 garlic cloves, minced and 1/2 teaspoons red pepper flakes and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
5. Deglaze: Pour in 1/2 cups dry white wine (e.g. Pinot Grigio) and 1/3 cups chicken or seafood broth, scraping up any browned bits. Simmer until slightly reduced, about 2–3 minutes. Add the juice from 1 lemon, juiced.
6. Bring it together: Add the cooked pasta and shrimp back to the pan. Toss to coat, adding reserved pasta water a splash at a time to loosen the sauce as needed. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Serve: Plate immediately, topped with 1/3 cups fresh parsley, chopped and 1/3 cups parmesan, grated (optional) if using. Serve with 1 lemon, sliced (for serving) on the side.

notes=
- Use fresh, large shrimp (16/20 count) for the best texture. Frozen works great — just thaw fully and pat very dry.
- Don't skip reserving pasta water — the starch helps the sauce cling to the noodles.
- Substitute wine with extra broth and a splash more lemon juice if preferred.
- For extra richness, swirl in 1–2 more tbsp of cold butter at the very end (mounting the sauce).
