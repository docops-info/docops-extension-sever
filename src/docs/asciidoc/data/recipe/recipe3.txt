title=Root Vegetables with Salted Fish
yield=Serves 4 to 6
prep=20 min
cook=20 min
theme=istanbul
tags=root vegetables, tropical, Guyanese, savory
summary=A tropical root vegetable dish with cassava, plantains, potatoes, and optional salted fish. It is seasoned with garlic, onion, tomato, and peppers for a savory finish.

ingredients=
- 1 cassava, peeled and cut into bite-size pieces
- 1 plantain, peeled and cut into bite-size pieces
- 1 potato, peeled and cut into bite-size pieces
- 1 white sweet potato, peeled and cut into bite-size pieces (optional)
- 1 green bell pepper, chopped
- 3 to 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 onion, chopped
- 1 tablespoon oil
- 1 chopped tomato
- salt to taste
- salted fish, optional
- scotch bonnet or habanero pepper, optional

steps=
1. Peel and cut the cassava, plantains, potatoes, and sweet potato into bite-size pieces.
2. Place the vegetables in a pot and boil until tender, about 7 minutes.
3. Strain the vegetables and set them aside.
4. In a pan, add oil, onions, peppers, and garlic.
5. Let the onions soften and start to caramelize.
6. Add the garlic and tomatoes to the pan.
7. Add the vegetables to the pan and stir them in the oil, onion, and garlic mixture.
8. Let the vegetables fry for about 2 minutes, then add salt and turn off the heat.
9. If using salted fish, wash it well, boil it, discard the water, and boil again until the salt is reduced.
10. Add the salted fish to the pan after the garlic and tomato step, then mix it into the aromatics before adding the vegetables.
11. For extra heat, add scotch bonnet, habanero, or another chili pepper during the garlic and tomato step.
12. Serve warm.

notes=
- Use less pepper if you want a milder flavor.
- The fish should be boiled more than once if it is still too salty.
- This dish works well as a side or a main meal.
- Serve with rice, roti, or enjoy it on its own.
